Why the Energy Body Comes First

[This is placeholder text showing how a blog post will look once published. Replace everything below with your own writing — the layout, spacing, and formatting will stay the same.]

Most students arrive at their first class expecting to work with the mind — to quiet their thoughts, to "switch off," to find some kind of mental silence. It's a reasonable expectation. Almost everything people read about meditation focuses on the mind.

In practice, though, the mind is rarely where the work begins. Long before thoughts settle, the body has its own kind of noise — tension held in the shoulders, a restlessness in the legs, a subtle current of energy that's either blocked or scattered. Trying to quiet the mind while this remains unaddressed is a bit like trying to read in a room with the lights flickering.

Starting where the body already is

This is why early sessions spend so much time on posture, breath, and simple body awareness. It can feel slow, even uneventful. But this is the groundwork that makes everything afterward possible. The energy body — the layer of experience that sits between the purely physical and the purely mental — is where most of this early work happens.

"The mind follows the energy. Settle one, and the other tends to follow."

What this looks like in a class

In a typical Foundations class, you'll notice more time spent on grounding, breath awareness, and gentle movement than you might expect from a "meditation" class. This isn't a warm-up before the real practice — it is the practice, at this stage.

As the energy body becomes more settled and familiar, the mind naturally has less to react to. What looked like "meditation" from the outside — sitting quietly with eyes closed — starts to feel different from the inside. That shift is usually the first sign that something real is beginning.

Where to go from here

If this resonates, the best next step isn't more reading — it's a class. Foundations of Stillness runs weekly and is open to complete beginners.
